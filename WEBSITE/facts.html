Interesting facts about India

  1. A floating post office

    2. India has the largest postal network in the world with over 1, 55,015 post offices. A single post office on an average serves a population of 7,175 people. The floating post office in Dal Lake, Srinagar, was inaugurated in August 2011.



                           
  2. Population in Mumbai

    3. There are roughly the same number of people leaving in Mumbai as there are in the whole of Australia!



                           
  3. Bandra Worli Sealink has steel wires equal to the earth's circumference

    4. It took a total of 2,57,00,000 man hours for completion and also weighs as much as 50,000 African elephants. A true engineering and architectural marvel.



                           
  4. Water on the moon was discovered by India

    5. In September 2009, India's ISRO Chandrayaan- 1 using its Moon Mineralogy Mapper detected water on the moon for the first time.



                           
  5. Science day in Switzerland is dedicated to Ex-Indian President, APJ Abdul Kalam

    6. The father of India's missile programme had visited Switzerland back in 2006. Upon his arrival, Switzerland declared May 26th as Science Day.



                           
  6. India's first President only took 50% of his salary

    7. When Dr Rajendra Prasad was appointed the President of India, he only took 50% of his salary, claiming he did not require more than that. Towards the end of his 12-year tenure he only took 25% of his salary. The salary of the President was Rs 10,000 back then.



                           
  7. The first rocket in India was transported on a cycle

    8. The first rocket was so light and small that it was transported on a bicycle to the Thumba Launching Station in Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala.



                           
  8. India has a spa just for elephants

    9. Elephants receive baths, massages and even food at the Punnathoor Cotta Elephant Yard Rejuvenation Centre in Kerala. Now that's a BIG step for the country.



                           
  9. The human calculator

    10. Shakuntla Devi was given this title after she demonstrated the calculation of two 13 digit numbers: 7,686,369,774,870 multiplied by 2,465,099,745,779 which were picked at random. She answered correctly within 28 seconds.



                           
  10. Dhyan Chand was offered German citizenship

    11. After defeating Germany 8-1 in the 1936 Berlin Olympics, Major Dhyan Chand, the wizard of hockey, was summoned by Hitler. He was promised German citizenship, a high post in the German military and the chance to play for the German national side. Dhyan Chand however declined the offer.



                           
  11. Havell's is purely an Indian brand & named after its first owner

    12. Though the company was bought for just 10 lakh Rupees a long time ago and is now a multi-billion electrical goods company, it's an Indian company and is still named after its original owner, Haveli Ram Gupta.



                           
  12. Diamonds were first mined in India

    13. Initially, diamonds were only found in the alluvial deposits in Guntur and Krishna District of the Krishna River Delta. Until diamonds were found in Brazil during the 18th century, India led the world in diamond production.
